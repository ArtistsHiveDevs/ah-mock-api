## 2. What data do we collect?

Depending on the type of user:

**Artists, musicians and bands:** stage name, email, phone number, city, music genre, career history, technical rider, bank details for payments, multimedia content (photos, videos, links). For participation in open calls and events that require it: demographic data (age, gender, city of origin, ethnic group), blood type, allergies and dietary restrictions. These last items are voluntary and sensitive; their purpose is to support emergency care, hospitality management at events, and compliance with population characterization requirements imposed by public or cultural funding calls.

**Venues, cultural spaces and establishments:** name, tax ID, representative's name, address, capacity, available equipment, contracting modalities and services offered (sound, lighting, hospitality, catering, accommodation, etc.).

**Cultural centers, festivals and event organizers:** organization name, tax ID or identifier, representative's name, active open calls, participation requirements and selection criteria.

**Promoters, agencies, brands and other music industry agents:** company or trade name, tax ID, contact details, area of expertise and geographic reach.

**All users:** login credentials, IP address, activity history on the platform and internal chat messages.

---

## 3. What do we use your data for?

- Creating and managing your account on the platform.
- Facilitating connections between artists and venues (booking requests, negotiations, confirmations).
- Streamlining and centralizing open call processes for cultural centers, venues, festivals and events.
- Processing payments and generating contractual documents.
- Operating the messaging and ratings system.
- Managing demographic data required by public or cultural entities for the characterization of their beneficiary population.
- Supporting hospitality management and emergency care during events (allergies, dietary restrictions, blood type), when the user has provided this information.
- Sending service notifications and platform updates.
- Improving our services through statistical usage analysis.
- Fulfilling legal and tax obligations.
- Developing new features to generate value for users, such as artist management tools, performance analytics, visibility of industry opportunities and access to music ecosystem resources.

We do not sell personal data to third parties.

## 5. Your rights (Law 1581 of 2012)

You have the right to:

- **Know** what data we hold about you and how we use it.
- **Update or correct** inaccurate or outdated information.
- **Request deletion** of your data when there is no legal obligation to retain it.
- **Withdraw consent** to the processing of your data.
- **File complaints** with the Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio (SIC) if you believe your rights have been violated.
- **Access your personal data free of charge** at any time.

---

## 6. How to exercise your rights

Write to us at **privacidad@artisthive.co** stating your full name, ID number, the right you wish to exercise and a description of your request.

**Response times:**
- Information requests: maximum **10 business days**.
- Claims (update, correction, deletion or withdrawal): maximum **15 business days**.

If a response within that period is not possible, we will inform you of the reasons and a new date, which will not exceed 5 or 8 additional business days respectively.

## 7. Sensitive data and minors

**Sensitive data:** Artist Hive collects, with the user's express consent, limited health data — blood type, allergies and dietary restrictions — solely to ensure the artist's safety during events and to facilitate hospitality management. For open calls that require it, demographic data such as ethnic identity or disability status may be collected, in accordance with population characterization criteria established by public or cultural entities. Providing these data is always voluntary; declining to do so does not affect access to the platform's other services.

**Minors:** Artist Hive's services are intended for users aged 18 and over. Registration of minors requires written authorization from a parent or legal guardian.
